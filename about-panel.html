Italian Limes is a research project and an interactive installation that explores the most remote Alpine regions, where national borders drift with glaciers.

The project focuses on the effects of climate change on shrinking ice sheets and the consequent shifts of the watershed that defines the national territories of Italy, Austria, Switzerland and France.

Investigating the fragile balance of the Alpine ecosystem, Italian Limes shows how natural frontiers are subject to the complexity of continuous ecological processes and their political enactment, depending on the technologies and norms that are used to represent them.

The project explores the condition of post-Schengen Europe, where borders are intended to fade away, but are simultaneously claimed by governments as the ultimate defence of the nation state. In the past few years, a network of apparently dormant 19th-century frontiers woke up from the dream of a borderless continent and materialised into a 21st-century psychosis of police checks, barbed wire fences, migrant encampments, proxy sovereignties and displaced jurisdictions.

Italian Limes aims to monitor the shifts in the Austrian-Italian watershed as an inquiry into the relation of borders and their representation. A grid of autonomous measurement units—installed at 3,300m above sea level in the midst of the Ã–tztal Alps, tracks the daily movement of the Austrian-Italian frontier and broadcasts its data to a drawing machine, printing a real-time representation of the movable border.

Originally commissioned as part of the 14th International Architecture Exhibition, la Biennale di Venezia, the project has been further developed in 2016 thorugh a joint scientific campaign with the Italian Glaciological Committee. The project has been developed after a research work in the archives of the Istituto Geografico Militare in Florence.

Italian Limes is currently part of Reset Modernity!, an exhibition curated by the philosopher Bruno Latour and AIME team at ZKM—Center for Art and Media, in Karlsuhe, Germany. The exhibition inaugurated on April 16th and will be open until August 21st, 2016.

Since its debut, the project has been highly acclaimed on traditional media and digital outlets.
