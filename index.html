Italian Limes is an ongoing research project and an interactive installation that explores the most remote Alpine regions, where borders drift with glaciers.

The project focuses on the effects of climate change on shrinking ice sheets and the consequent shifts of the watershed line that defines the national territories of Italy, Austria, Switzerland and France.

A grid of autonomous measurement units—installed at 3,600m above sea level, tracks the daily movement of the Austrian-Italian border and broadcasts its data to a drawing machine, printing a real-time representation of the movable border.

Investigating the fragile balance of the Alpine ecosystem, Italian Limes shows how natural frontiers are subject to the complexity of continuous ecological processes and their political enactment, depending on the technologies and norms that are used to represent them. The project explores the condition of post-Schengen Europe, where borders are intended to fade away, but are simultaneously claimed by governments as the ultimate defence of the nation state.

Originally commissioned as part of the 14th International Architecture Exhibition, la Biennale di Venezia, the installation developed in 2016 for ZKM Karlsruhe is the result of a joint scientific campaign with the Italian Glaciological Committee.

In 2015 Italian Limes was exhibited at the South Tyrol Museum of Archaeology in Bozen. In 2016 it will be part of an important exhibition curated by the philosopher Bruno Latour at ZKM — Center for Art and Media, in Karlsuhe, Germany.

Founded in 1989, the ZKM | Center for Art and Media Karlsruhe is a cultural institution which, since 1997, has been located in a historical industrial building in Karlsruhe, Germany that formerly housed a munitions factory. The ZKM organizes special exhibitions and thematic events, carries out research projects, produces works in the field of new media and offers public as well as individualized communications and educational programs.

Since its debut, the project has been highly acclaimed on traditional media and digital outlets.
